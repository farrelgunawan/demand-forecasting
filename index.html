4 · Method

A Holt-Winters additive model (α=0.1, β=0.1, γ=0.1, tuned by backtest) was selected after comparing it against Naive, Seasonal-Naive, 3-month Moving Average, and a Linear Regression with trend, seasonal, and lag-12 features. Prediction intervals come from the empirical distribution of backtest errors at each horizon. All models were implemented from scratch in NumPy.

⚠️ Note on data: this demonstration uses a synthetic dataset that mimics a melamine-tableware demand pattern (trend + Lebaran/year-end seasonality + noise). The methodology is identical to what would run on real, sanitized factory data.
